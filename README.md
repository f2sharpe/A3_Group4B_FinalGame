Description
Our game, titled “Focus Frenzy,” is designed to give the player a sense of what living with ADHD is like. This game specifically focuses on task completion and how it can be incredibly difficult for those with ADHD to finish tasks. Oftentimes, those with ADHD get easily distracted, frustrated, and struggle with completing tasks [2; 3]. Focus Frenzy gives players short tasks to complete in the form of typing in a specific key sequence, but players are also chased by distractions that can lead to trouble finishing tasks, trouble completing levels, and feeling overwhelmed/frustrated.
Complete all the tasks on all 3 levels to beat the game. Good luck!

Setup and Interaction Instructions
Music starts playing if you press any key on your keyboard while on the splash screen.
Press SPACE to begin playing.
Move with WASD keys.
Locate the task boxes with your character and position your character over the tasks to begin them.
Type in the correct key sequence to finish the task.
“Distractions” in the form of red circles will chase you. They will drain your focus bar if they catch you.
Distractions move faster towards you when you focus on tasks.
There are power-ups that you can collect to regain some of your focus, but you need to be fast enough to catch them.
Safe spaces will spawn in occasionally to give you temporary protection from distractions.
Goal: Complete all the tasks before your focus runs out in order to move to the next level.

Iteration Notes
Post-playtest: Three changes made based on playtesting
Update typing mechanics so it’s clearer to a player when they make an error. Have the word “flash” with red and then make an error sound.
Implement an obvious screen change for when damage is being taken. Have red flashes on the screen that indicate damage lost.
Indicate power-ups more clearly so players know what they do. The first couple times they appear have a tag above it that says “collect power-up!” (like in a tutorial).
