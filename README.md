Description
Our game, titled “Focus Frenzy,” is designed to give the player a sense of what living with ADHD is like. This game specifically focuses on task completion and how it can be incredibly difficult for those with ADHD to finish tasks. Oftentimes, those with ADHD get easily distracted, frustrated, and struggle with completing tasks [2; 3]. Focus Frenzy gives players short tasks to complete in the form of typing in a specific key sequence, but players are also chased by distractions that can lead to trouble finishing tasks, trouble completing levels, and feeling overwhelmed/frustrated.
Complete all the tasks on all 3 levels to beat the game. Good luck!

Setup and Interaction Instructions
Press SPACE to begin playing.
Move with WASD keys.
Locate the task boxes with your character and position your character over the tasks to begin them.
Press the correct key sequence to finish the task.
“Distractions” in the form of red circles will chase you. They will drain your focus bar if they catch you.
Distractions move faster towards you when you focus on tasks.
There are power-ups that you can collect to regain some of your focus, but you need to be fast enough to catch them.
Goal: Complete all the tasks before your focus runs out in order to move to the next level.

Iteration Notes
Post-playtest: Three changes made based on playtesting
We changed the entire design of our game to be simpler. Initially, we had a more complicated concept that involved mini games, but we changed it to its current state to be easier to design and understand.
We changed the game mechanics to create a different emotional outcome. Originally, we were focusing more on the inability to finish tasks, but we changed the main emotional outcome to be overwhelm, stress, and frustration by avoiding distractions/enemies and not being able to finish the level because of the overwhelm.
We added camera shake when the player hits the distractions/enemies, so the harmful impact would be more obvious. The player might not have been able to see the focus bar draining as much before, so we wanted to make it clear that it was causing them harm.
Post-showcase: Two planned improvements based on feedback, and two planned improvements based on our own notes.
Based on feedback, we are going to be implementing a feature that makes the character’s “expression” change as they move through the game. As their focus decreases, they will get more upset; as they complete tasks, they will look more focused; when they collect powerups, they will be happy again, etc.
Based on feedback, we want to hone in more on the typing game aspect, and use actual words for the player to type as opposed to random letter combinations.
We want to implement a feature that allows players to exit tasks. Realistically, individuals with ADHD are able to walk away from tasks, but they get easily distracted and have a hard time going back to finish the task. Implementing this feature would allow the player to walk away from a task, but the longer they delay the completion of it, the more distractions spawn in and the harder the level is to complete.
We want to implement a “safe space” on the map. This would appear as a flower or some other distinguishable shape, and would protect the player from the distractions chasing them. The safe space would only allow the player to stay in there for a certain amount of time (5-7 seconds), and would gradually shrink before disappearing to show the countdown. This safe space would also slowly regenerate the player’s health, but it has a cooldown time of 5 seconds (roughly) before respawning.
